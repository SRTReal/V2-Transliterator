Formation of Adverbs

Suffix What it Denotes Examples Additional Information
θε, θεν Movement from a place or thing

[ABLATIVE]		 οὐρανόθεν 'from the heavens'
ἠῶθεν 'from dawn, at dawn'
ἐγγύθεν 'from nearby'
πάντοθεν 'from all sides'
τηλόθεν 'from afar'
οὐδετέρωθεν 'from neither side'		 For o-stems: Simply add -θεν after the o and apply an oxeia to it, e.g. παιδιόθεν 'from early age' παιδίον 'young child'


For ᾱ-stems: As you know in Ionic the ᾱ is replaced by η, thus this occurs and -θεν is affixed to it e.g. Κρήτηθεν 'from Crete' Κρήτη 'Crete'.
Although note that if words have the accent on the final η, e.g. in εὐνή 'bed' | ἀγορή 'marketplace' | ἀρχή 'beginning | then this accent becomes a perispomene, e.g. εὐνῆθεν 'from bed'


For consonantal stems: Simply add -ό-θεν, e.g. χειμωνόθεν 'from a storm' χειμών 'storm'
Note that if the noun has a long vowel on the ultima, e.g. μήτηρ 'mother' | πατήρ 'father' | φρήν 'mind' | then it must become a short vowel before affixation in the form of the root, e.g. μητρόθεν 'from the mother'
δε, ζε Movement to or towards a place or thing

[ALLATIVE]		 Οὔλυμπόνδε 'to Olympus' | θύραζε 'towards the door(s) | πέδονδε 'to the ground; earthwards' | πτόλεμονδε 'to war' Simply affix -δε to the accusative form, -ζε appears when -σδε, e.g. Ἀθήναζε 'to Athens'
NUANCE: Expresses movement towards a specific goal as opposed to -σε which expresses movement in some direction
θι In or at a place or thing

[LOCATIVE]		 κεῖθι 'at that (place)' | ἠῶθι 'at dawn' | Κορινθόθι 'at Corinth' | οὐρανόθι 'in the heavens' | μεσσόθι 'in the middle' | οὐδετέρωθι 'in neither place' Simply affix -θι in most cases, only removing the final consonant. If the ultima of the noun contains a long vowel, a perispomene is placed upon it
Note that if the noun ends in a consonant, a linking vowel 'o' is required, where the oxeia is placed on it e.g. κηρόθι 'in the heart, heartily' κῆρ 'heart'
σε Movement to or towards a place or thing

[ALLATIVE]		 τηλόσε 'to a distance, far away' | οὐδετέρωσε 'to neither of 2 sides, neither way' | ἀμφοτέρωσε 'in both directions, to both sides' | πάντοσε 'to every way, in all directions' NUANCE: Expresses movement in some direction as opposed to -δε which expresses movement towards a specific goal, hence why formed derived from ἀμφοτέρωσε and οὐδετέρωσε are not present in -δε
δις [ALLATIVE] οἴκαδις 'towards home' | χαμάδις 'to the ground' Doric dialectal equivalent of the affix -δε
οῖ [FOSSILISED LOCATIVE]
[OLD DATIVE]		 πεδοῖ 'on the ground/earth' | οἴκοι 'at home' | ἰσθμοῖ 'on or at the Isthmus | Seem to have been old datives, later becoming οἴκῳ, πεδῷ, ἰσθμῷ, with the ἐν being understood
σι [FOSSILISED LOCATIVE]
[OLD DATIVE]		 Ὀλυμπίασι 'at Olympia' | Πλαταιᾶσι 'at Plataea' Originally datives plural in Ionic, later became annexed to other names in α
χη, χου [FOSSILISED LOCATIVE]
[OLD DATIVE]		 πανταχῆ 'everywhere, on all sides, in every direction' | πανταχοῦ 'everywhere' supply the place of the preposition ἐν
θα [FOSSILISED LOCATIVE]
[OLD DATIVE]		 ἔνθα 'to there, there, to where, where' | ἐνθαῦτα 'to here, to there, in this' supply the place of the preposition ἐν
